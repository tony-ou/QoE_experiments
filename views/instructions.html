Instructions for the test

The goal of the experiment is to study how video impairments at different location of the video damage viewers' experience differently. Your job is to watch a list of test videos with same content but impairments at different location, and judge how much downgraded the test video is from the reference video. Please pay close attention to the following guidelines:

  • The test videos are created by inserting impairments (next page contains examples of impairment ) to a reference video at different locations. You will watch the reference video first.
  • You will grade the videos based on how downgraded they're from the reference videos, by choosing from 5 options: Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor, Bad. To make grading easier, we will show you what best and worst quality means. The reference video, which you see first, will be "excellent" quality. The second video you will see should be "bad".
  • You might think reference video has quality issues as well. But these are not concerns of this experiment. We only wish to measure how the additional impairment affect the viewing experience.
  • On the grading page, you will also be asked to select what impairments you saw in the test video. (This is an attention test).
  • Please be sure to watch each video in its full length. The button to the next page appears after you finish the video. And please don't refresh video page. If you have to because video fails to load, please report on the last page.

Please take the simple survey before the test.
