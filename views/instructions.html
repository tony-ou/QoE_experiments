Instructions for the test

In this test, you will watch a series of test videos (~20 seconds each) created from a reference video by adding additional quality impairments at different timestamps. You are going to rate how the impairments in the video affect the video quality and your streaming experience. Note the goal of the experiment is not to gauge the test videos' absolute quality but their relative quality downgrade in comparison the reference videos. Please pay close attention to the following guidelines:

  • An impairment can be any temporal or spatial distortion of the reference video. Some examples are bluring, stalling, tiling, etc. These might or might not be present in the actual test videos.
  • You will watch the reference video first and you must rate it as Imperceptible Impairment. (This is the an attention test) Again, this doesn't mean the reference video is in perfect quality, but just that there's no additional downgrade.
  • On the grading page, besides answering how the impairments impact your streaming experience, you will also be asked to select what additional impairments you saw in the test video. (This is an attention test. There is a correct answer and that should be obvious as long as you paid attention to the difference btw test and reference videos.)
  • Please be sure to watch each video in its full length. We can tell if you skip a video. And please don't refresh the video page when watching. If you have to refresh because network fails, be sure to report this on the last page.
  • The survey will end with a question like "what is the quality difference between the test videos". You must give a reasonable answer such as by mentioning what impairments you saw and describing why you rated some test videos higher than others in general language. (meaning you don't have to recollect details of the videos)

Please take the simple survey before the test.
